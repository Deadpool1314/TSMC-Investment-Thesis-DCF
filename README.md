**Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (NYSE: TSM / TWSE: 2330) Quarterly DCF Valuation Model & Investment Thesis**

A quarterly-cadence DCF valuation model and buy-side style equity research report on TSMC, built from primary filings and public data. This research covers TSMC's fundamental valuation, competitive positioning, geopolitical risk framework, scenario analysis anchored to discount rate and market re-rating assumptions, and a detailed assessment of why the market's apparent discount to intrinsic value is dynamic rather than static.

**Model Overview**

•	Quarterly financial model spanning 5 projected quarters (Jun-26F through Jun-27F), denominated in New Taiwan Dollars (NTD).

•	Revenue disaggregated across 7 process node categories (3nm through 0.25μm and above), with differentiated growth assumptions reflecting AI/HPC demand acceleration at leading nodes versus mature node stabilization.

•	Cross-currency peer WACC build using 5 comparable foundry operators across KRX, NASDAQ, and TWSE (Samsung, Intel, SMIC, GlobalFoundries, UMC), with beta un-levering/re-levering across multi-currency capital structures.

•	Blended WACC: 8.8% | Terminal Growth Rate: 2.5%

•	Detailed bond and bank loan maturity schedule tracking individual domestic and overseas tranches with weighted average interest rates.

•	Full integrated Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement with balance sheet check across all projected periods.

•	CapEx-linked contractor payable dynamics modelled within operating cash flow.

**Investment Thesis Summary**

Rating: Buy

DCF Intrinsic Value: ~NTD 2,653/share (Base Case)

Market Price: NTD 2,350/share (as of 1st June 26)

DCF-Implied Discount: 12-22% (see methodology note below)

The thesis argues that TSMC's persistent valuation discount, despite dominant foundry market share and irreplaceable strategic positioning within global semiconductor supply chains, stems from three structural forces operating independently of business quality:

1. Geopolitical Discounting

Cross-strait risk creates a persistent institutional discount on Taiwan-domiciled assets. The thesis argues this discount is partially overstated relative to how systemically irreplaceable TSMC is - a forced disruption to TSMC's operations would cascade through the global technology supply chain in ways no government or investor can fully hedge. Geographic fab diversification across the U.S. (Arizona), Japan (Kumamoto), and Europe (Dresden) is gradually reducing concentration risk over a multi-year horizon.

2. Expansion-Stage Capital Intensity

Despite nearly five decades of operation, TSMC continues to deploy capital at expansion-stage intensity - 2nm node development, advanced packaging buildout, and multi-geography fab construction compress near-term free cash flow, making TSMC appear more expensive on FCF-based multiples than earnings multiples suggest.

3. Institutional Capital Rotation

TSMC is a long-duration compounding story in a market environment that has been rewarding short-duration returns.
This structural mismatch creates a temporary but persistent discount that narrows, as the investment cycle matures and earnings power becomes visible. 
Competitive moat durability is evaluated against Samsung and Intel across EUV lithography access, advanced node yield rates, and customer concentration. TSMC's relationships with NVIDIA, Apple, AMD, and Qualcomm are assessed as effectively irreplaceable over any 3–5-year investment horizon.

**Scenario Analysis**

Why Operational Scenarios Are Not Used?
TSMC's risk profile is asymmetrically external rather than operational. Demand consistently exceeds capacity, management has not materially missed its own guidance in over a decade, competitive threats at leading nodes remain years away from being credible, and customer concentration risk is mitigated by the absence of any alternative supplier at 3nm and below. Changing revenue growth, margin, or cost assumptions to construct bear/base/bull cases would manufacture operational risk that does not exist in the business with any realistic near-term probability.
Scenarios are therefore constructed at the valuation and discount rate level, which is where TSMC's genuine uncertainty actually sits.

**DCF-Based Scenarios (WACC + TGR)**

These scenarios are derived directly from the sensitivity table in the model. No operational assumptions change - only the cost of capital and terminal growth rate reflect various macroeconomic and geopolitical environments.

Bear rationale: 
Assuming a 10.5% WACC and 1.5% terminal growth, the intrinsic value is estimated at NT$1,942.4 per share, representing approximately 17.3% downside relative to the prevailing market price.
This case Assumes cross-strait tensions escalate, institutional investors apply higher Taiwan country risk premium, interest rate environment stays higher for longer, AI capex cycle peaks earlier than consensus expect.

Base rationale: 
Using an 8.8% WACC and 2.5% terminal growth, produces an intrinsic value of NT$2,653.7 per share, implying approximately 12.9% upside.
This reflects Current geopolitical discount persists but does not worsen, Fed cutting cycle partially offsets Taiwan risk premium, AI/HPC demand sustains through projection horizon.

Bull rationale: 
Assumeing 7.5% WACC and 3.0% terminal growth yield an intrinsic value of NT$3,634.4 per share, representing approximately 54.7% upside.
Geopolitical discount compresses as Arizona and Japan fabs demonstrably de-risk Taiwan concentration, US-China tensions ease at the margin, AI infrastructure buildout extends beyond current consensus cycle length.

**Market Price Target Scenarios (P/E Based)**

These scenarios address what the market could re-rate TSMC to under different multiple environments - distinct from DCF intrinsic value, which represents fundamental worth regardless of market sentiment.
The multiple chosen in each scenario reflects where TSMC sits on the spectrum between commodity foundry and quality technology infrastructure asset - a spectrum directly determined by how the market resolves the geopolitical discount question.

Bear rationale: 
Assumes a 16x forward P/E, resulting in a target price of NT$1,869.2 per share, representing approximately 20.5% downside from the current market price.
This reflects geopolitical risk deepens, market treats TSMC as a Taiwan-risk asset rather than a technology moat asset, multiple compresses toward commodity semiconductor peers.

Base rationale:
Applies a 22x forward P/E, generating a target price of NT$2,570.2 per share, or approximately 9.4% upside.
Assumes the market continues assigning a moderate geopolitical discount while recognising TSMC's strong competitive position and earnings quality.

Bull rationale:
Assumes a 26x forward P/E, implying a target price of NT$3,037.5 per share, representing approximately 29.3% upside.
This scenario assumes geopolitical discount compresses materially as geographic diversification is recognised by the market, AI cycle extends, TSMC re-rates toward quality technology infrastructure multiple consistent with its actual competitive 
position.

Methodology - Forward Ratio Calculation

Forward ratios use the NTM (Next Twelve Months) convention, consistent with Bloomberg institutional standard:

Jun-26 NTM = Q2 Jun-26F + Q3 Sep-26F + Q4 Dec-26F + Q1 Mar-27F

Sep-26 NTM = Q3 Sep-26F + Q4 Dec-26F + Q1 Mar-27F + Q2 Jun-27F

Ratios are NOT calculated by multiplying a single quarter by 4. TSMC exhibits clear seasonal patterns and quarterly revenue 
ramp - uniform annualization would materially distort the output.

**Valuation Ratios**

The forward valuation profile becomes progressively more attractive over the forecast horizon as earnings continue to expand faster than enterprise value. Forward P/E declines from 20.1x (June 2026 NTM) to 17.9x (September 2026 NTM), primarily reflecting accelerating AI and HPC-driven earnings growth rather than a change in market valuation. Similarly, EV/Revenue compresses from 10.0x to 9.0x as revenue growth outpaces the relatively stable enterprise value. Price-to-Free Cash Flow declines from 26.2x to 23.6x, remaining elevated due to TSMC operating near the peak of its capital expenditure cycle, where CapEx is approximately 30% of revenue. This should not be interpreted as a deterioration in cash generation, but rather as a consequence of heavy investment in advanced manufacturing capacity. Free cash flow per share is projected to increase from NT$89.6 to NT$99.6, indicating continued improvement in cash generation despite elevated capital expenditure.

Note: EV/EBITDA excluded - EBITDA is approximated as EBIT + 70–80% of SG&A as D&A is not separately disclosed at quarterly level. This approximation is not precise enough to report as a standalone valuation metric. Noted in model.

CapEx/Revenue - Assumption Rationale: 

Capital expenditure is projected to gradually moderate throughout the forecast period, declining from 30% of revenue in June and September 2026 to 29% during December 2026 and March 2027, before reaching 28% by June 2027. Relative to TSMC's historical average of approximately 32%, this represents a gradual reduction of 2–4 percentage points.

Projected CapEx/Revenue declines modestly from the historical average of ~32%, reflecting:
1.	2nm (N2) node development substantially complete as of 2026, with volume production ramping - transitioning from capital-intensive R&D and pilot line phase to manufacturing yield optimisation, which carries materially lower incremental CapEx intensity.

2.	TSMC's platform coverage is effectively comprehensive across mobile, HPC/AI, automotive, IoT, and networking - limiting the likelihood of greenfield platform entry requiring new categories of capital expenditure. Future CapEx is modelled as capacity expansion within known nodes rather than structural new platform investment.

3.	Revenue growing faster than incremental capacity cost as fabs already under construction (Arizona Fab 21, Kumamoto) ramp toward fuller utilisation.

Key assumption risk: Advanced packaging capacity (CoWoS, SoIC) for AI accelerator demand remains severely supply-constrained. Aggressive expansion here could partially offset node development CapEx decline, keeping the ratio at the higher end of the 28–30% projected range rather than declining toward 28% by Jun-27F.

Note: Sub-2nm node development (A16, 1.4nm) is at early R&D stage and is not assumed to materially impact the projection period CapEx envelope, as volume production for these nodes falls outside the Jun-27F model horizon.

**Model Sensitivity & Valuation Date Considerations**

The DCF-implied discount to market price is sensitive to cash flow timing assumptions within each quarter:

•	Valuation as of 1st May 2026, cash flows at quarter midpoint: implied discount ~22% to intrinsic value (~NTD 2,653.7/share)

•	Reframed as of 1st June 2026, cash flows at quarter end: implied discount compresses to ~12–14%

This sensitivity to a purely mechanical assumption - rather than any change in business fundamentals - indicates the precise discount figure should be interpreted as a range (12–22%) rather than a point estimate.Additionally, as the valuation date ages, near-term cash flows move closer in time and are penalised less by discounting. The apparent gap between market price and intrinsic value naturally compresses over time even without a change in stock price or business performance. This suggests the market may have been progressively and correctly pricing TSMC closer to intrinsic value as uncertainty around near-term cash flows resolves quarter by quarter — consistent with efficient market incorporation of information rather than a sustained valuation error.A discount that mechanically narrows with the passage of time is not the same as a catalyst-driven re-rating. These two 
should not be conflated when assessing whether a Buy recommendation remains valid.
